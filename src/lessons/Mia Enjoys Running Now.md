Mia is 22 years old, but she still doesn't have a 'driver’s license'. Her friends often tease her, but she 'doesn’t mind'. She loves walking and enjoys 'spending time' outside. Every day, she walks to school and feels 'calm' and happy.

1: How old is Mia?  
2: Does Mia have a driver's license?  
3: Do her friends tease her?  
4: Does she mind?  
5: What does Mia love?

Mia walks to the market when she needs 'groceries'. She does not  'get tired' easily and finds walking relaxing. This 'activity' helps her feel good. She walks for hours and enjoys the quiet streets, 'even though' some days are hot.

{% audioPlayer "/audio/mia-enjoys-running-now2.mp3" %}

1: Where does Mia go for groceries?  
2: Does she get tired easily?  
3: How does walking make her feel?  
4: How long does she walk?  
5: What does she enjoy?

Running is not the same for Mia. When she runs quickly, her stomach hurts. She does not like running, though her friends say it is good for her. She thinks walking is better and does not want to run.

{% audioPlayer "/audio/mia-enjoys-running-now3.mp3" %}

1: Does Mia like running?  
2: What happens when she runs?  
3: What do her friends say?  
4: What does Mia think is better?

Mia has a new job far away. She does not plan to learn how to drive.    'Instead', she plans to take the bus. One day, she was waiting at the bus stop, but the bus was late. It started to rain, and she had no umbrella.

{% audioPlayer "/audio/mia-enjoys-running-now4.mp3" %}

1: Where is Mia’s new job?  
2: Does she plan to learn to drive?  
3: How does she plan to go to work?  
4: What happened at the bus stop?  
5: Did she have an umbrella?

Mia decided to run home, even though she hated running. Something strange happened. She realized she was enjoying the run home. She ran faster and faster. She was surprised because her stomach did not hurt.

{% audioPlayer "/audio/mia-enjoys-running-now5.mp3" %}

1: Why did Mia run home?  
2: What strange thing happened?  
3: How did she feel while running?  
4: Did her stomach hurt?  
5: Was she surprised?

She finally reached home, smiling. "Maybe running isn’t bad," she thought. After that day, she 'started' running every morning. She didn’t need the bus 'anymore'. She loved her new 'ability' and felt happy when she 'finished'.
