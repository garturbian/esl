In the early years of the twentieth century, a group of men had traveled to Egypt to study an ancient tomb. The tomb had been hidden under the sand for many years. It belonged to a forgotten king from a distant time. The land around it was dry and empty. During the day, the sun was strong and bright. At night, the air became cold and still.


1: Where did the men travel?  
2: What did they want to study?  
3: How long had the tomb been hidden?  
4: Who did the tomb belong to?  
5: What was the land like?  
6: How was the weather in the day and at night?  

{% audioPlayer "/audio/the-egyptian-tomb_1.mp3" %}

The camp stood near the tomb. There were several tents, some tools, and wooden boxes for supplies. Workers moved slowly in the heat. They carried sand away from the entrance. The opening to the tomb was dark and quiet. It seemed to watch everything without sound.

1: Where was the camp?  
2: What things were in the camp?  
3: How did the workers move?  
4: What did they do with the sand?  
5: What was the tomb entrance like?  
6: How did the tomb feel to the people?

One of the leaders of the work had been Sir John Willard. He had been a serious man who cared deeply about history. He had wanted to understand the past and share it with others. But soon after the tomb had been opened, he had died.

His son, Guy Willard, had stayed at the camp. He had taken his father’s place, but he did not feel ready. He walked through the camp with a worried face. He often looked toward the tomb as if it held a secret he could not understand.

2: What role did he take?

Another important man at the camp was Dr. Ames. He had been the doctor for the group. He had a calm voice and careful hands. When people felt sick, they went to him. He always said, "Do not worry. I will take care of you."

However, after Sir John’s death, other men had also died. Each death had come suddenly. Each time, Dr. Ames had been there to help, but the help had not been enough. People had begun to whisper about a curse.
